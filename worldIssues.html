Rural Areas of the World Struggle to Provide Access to Quality STEM Education

STEM EDUCATION

In rural and underserved areas, STEM education faces significant challenges due to limited access to resources. This includes inadequate technology, unreliable internet connectivity, and a shortage of updated learning materials. These obstacles hinder students' ability to engage fully in STEM subjects, ultimately impacting their future opportunities. Quantum Lane Softworks recognizes these issues and is committed to addressing them by providing technology solutions, supporting schools with necessary equipment, and offering educational software. To further combat this issue, Quantum Lane Softworks plans to collaborate with Elon Musk, leveraging his expertise and resources to bring cutting-edge technology and innovative solutions to these communities.

    Roadblocks in Access to STEM Education

    Issues with STEM in Rural Communities

    Rachel S. Harris'(2018), thesis addresses STEM education challenges in rural schools due to limited resources, inadequate funding, unqualified teachers, and cultural barriers. As a Georgia Southern University professor, Harris emphasizes the need to improve STEM education to meet global demands. Harris (2018), highlights the importance of STEM for economic growth, innovation, and job market preparation. Harris points out that outdated technology and insufficient STEM materials, along with less funding compared to urban and suburban schools, hinder STEM education in rural areas. Harris (2018), also discusses the difficulty in attracting qualified teachers due to lower salaries and fewer incentives. Cultural factors, such as lower expectations and support influenced by parents' educational backgrounds, further impede progress. Harris concludes that making STEM education relevant to local contexts can engage and support students and parents, stressing the urgency of resolving these issues to remain globally competitive and ensure equal education for all students.

    Rural Communities Need STEM Education

    The article by Congressman Frank Lucas(2019) emphasizes the urgent need for improved STEM education in rural America to remain globally competitive. Rural students face significant challenges like a lack of STEM resources and limited internet access. Lucas advocates for the Rural STEM Education Act to enhance teacher resources, hands-on learning, and broadband access.

    Systemic Issues in STEM Education

    This report from the United Nations discusses the problem with STEM Education in rural and underdeveloped countries in Africa. In the report the U.N. highlights lack of resources, including internet access as one of the many problems STEM Education encounters throughout Africa.
